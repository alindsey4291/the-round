About The Round

Not a church. Not therapy. Something more grounded.

The Round is a practice-based community for people who want spiritual depth without coercion, accountability without shame, and growth that actually sticks.

We are C.O.R.E Companions — people practicing Compassion, Open Faith, Reverence, and Empathy as daily disciplines. We gather in Circles, take our baselines, revise weekly, and build evidence that we are still in motion.

Founded by A.L. Lindsey — veteran, author, and community builder — The Round was built from lived experience: service, loss, repair, and the stubborn belief that as long as you are breathing, your story is not closed.

How we're different

  • Not A church or religious organization. Open Faith means no doctrine is required. Devout, doubtful, or undecided — all belong here.
  • Not A therapy group. Circles are not clinical. We do not diagnose, treat, or replace professional mental health care.
  • Not A course with a finish line. The Round is a way of life — a repeatable practice, not a program you complete.
  • Yes A community of people who take their own growth seriously — and want others around them who do the same.
  • Yes A structure that turns intention into evidence — daily, weekly, and monthly rhythms that make change visible.
  • Yes A place where you can be wherever you are — and still belong.

The Four Pillars

Not values to declare — skills to practice

C.O.R.E is a framework for becoming — four disciplines that show up differently depending on the day, and grow deeper depending on the commitment.

C

Compassion

Truth with warmth. Standards stay. Cruelty — toward yourself or others — goes. Repair without humiliation.

O

Open Faith

Freedom of conscience with curiosity. Devout, doubtful, or undecided — you belong here without pressure.

R

Reverence

Sacred dignity for every person — especially in disagreement. You speak about people as if they are in the room.

E

Empathy

Listening that changes behavior. Connection that becomes action. Not agreement — understanding that moves.

"As a C.O.R.E Companion, I practice compassion without denial, I keep faith open, I treat dignity as sacred, and I train empathy into my choices. I refuse premature verdicts — about myself or anyone else."

The Founder

A.L. Lindsey

A.L. Lindsey grew up in a military family, completed ROTC, joined the Army, and deployed in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom and Operation New Dawn. He learned what accountability looks like when it is not theoretical — when details matter because consequences are real.

His book, Nothing Is Final Until You're Dead, is the philosophy behind The Round. The ideas live in the book. The community is where they become practice.
