About The Round

The circle is the point

We gather in circles because circles train equality: no one sits at the head of the table — only shared dignity and shared responsibility.

The Round is a way of life and community made up of C.O.R.E Companions who follow C.O.R.E Circle thinking — a spiritual way of living built on Compassion, Open Faith, Reverence, and Empathy.

We believe people are unfinished, that mercy is accuracy, and that identity grows through practice — not just intention.

  • Not A church or denomination
  • Yes A spiritual community with Open Faith
  • Not Therapy or clinical treatment
  • Yes Structured practice with trained facilitation
  • Not A course you finish
  • Yes A way of life you keep practicing
  • Not Performance-based belonging
  • Yes Evidence-based growth, no perfection required

The Four Pillars

C.O.R.E

C
Compassion

Truth with warmth. Repair without humiliation. Standards stay; cruelty goes.

O
Open Faith

Freedom of conscience. Curiosity without coercion. Belonging without doctrinal gatekeeping.

R
Reverence

Sacred dignity for every person — especially in disagreement.

E
Empathy

Listening that changes behavior. Connection that becomes action.

The Book

The philosophy behind The Round

A book built around a simple philosophy: as long as you're alive, there is room. Room to learn. Room to repair. Room to reinterpret. Room to begin again without calling it failure.

Every chapter includes Field Notes and exercises, because ideas should be lived, not admired.

AL

A.L. Lindsey

Founder of The Round

A.L. Lindsey grew up in a military family, did ROTC, and joined the Army. He deployed in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom and Operation New Dawn, working in logistics and accountability — systems where details matter because consequences are real.

That experience taught him something he didn't expect to learn so personally: you can live a chapter that shapes you and still not be defined by it forever.

The Round grew from a simple conviction: people are unfinished, mercy is accuracy, and identity grows through practice — not just intention. C.O.R.E Think is designed to be repeatable, measurable, and deeply human.
