What is The Round

Where healing becomes habit, and habit becomes culture

The Round is a community of C.O.R.E Companions — people who have decided to stop calling their lives final. Together, we practice Compassion, Open Faith, Reverence, and Empathy as daily disciplines, not distant ideals. We are not a church. We are not a therapy group. We are something more grounded: a way of life shared with others who are still becoming.


Founded by A.L. Lindsey, The Round was built from lived experience — service, loss, repair, and the stubborn belief that as long as you are breathing, your story is not closed.

The Four Pillars

Not values to declare — skills to practice

C.O.R.E is not a creed. It is a framework for becoming — built on four disciplines that show up differently depending on the day, and grow deeper depending on the commitment.

C

Compassion

Truth with warmth. Standards stay. Cruelty — toward yourself or others — goes. Repair without humiliation.

O

Open Faith

Freedom of conscience with curiosity. You are spiritually grounded without requiring agreement. Certainty and doubt both belong here.

R

Reverence

Sacred dignity for every person — especially in disagreement. You speak about people as if they are in the room.

E

Empathy

Listening that changes behavior. Connection that becomes action. Not agreement — understanding that moves.

"As a C.O.R.E Companion, I practice compassion without denial, I keep faith open, I treat dignity as sacred, and I train empathy into my choices. I refuse premature verdicts — about myself or anyone else."

The Companion's Promise

How It Works

Built for real life, not ideal conditions

01

Take your baseline

Rate yourself honestly across all four pillars — 0 to 10, with evidence from your actual week. Not your best week. This one. That snapshot becomes your compass.

02

Begin the Daily 10

Ten minutes a day — two for each pillar — using the prompts and rhythms in your C.O.R.E Rule of Life. The bad-day version counts as much as the good-day version.

03

Join a Circle

Weekly small groups led by trained C.O.R.E Stewards. A place where you practice the pillars alongside others — not to perform, but to be seen and to grow.

The Book

The philosophy behind The Round

This is not a self-help book. It is a philosophical companion for people who are tired of being told who they are. It is built around one grounded insistence: as long as you are alive, there is room. Room to repair. Room to reinterpret. Room to begin again without calling it failure.


Every chapter connects to a practice in The Round. The ideas live in the book. The community is where they become real.

Stewardship

Lead with license

C.O.R.E Stewards are certified facilitators who protect inclusion, safety, and Open Faith within Circles. Stewardship is not a title. It is a practice in itself.

  • Eight-module certification curriculum
  • Steward Council — peer supervision and ongoing training
  • Circle kit license — script, agreements, brand materials
  • Rights to lead official Circles within The Round
  • Ethics and inclusion review
  • A community of leaders who take their work seriously
